👶 Early Security: Building Resilience

The Foundation of Healthy Development

  • Early care-giving relationship teaches children to know love
  • Attuned interactions develop brain pathways for social understanding
  • Secure attachment creates internal sense of being "worth loving"
  • Caregiver helps child understand their emotions and physical states
  • Resilience is built through repeated cycles of stress → comfort → security

✅ Secure Response

Child frightened by dog:

  • Caregiver rushes out to help
  • Picks up child, holds them tight
  • Speaks in calm, soothing voice
  • Stays until child is calm again

Result: Child learns the world can be safe

⚠️ Insecure Response

Same situation:

  • Caregiver grabs child by arm
  • Smacks and drags inside
  • Shouts and blames child
  • No comfort or soothing

Result: Child learns the world is threatening

⚡ Understanding Trauma

Definition of Trauma

Trauma occurs when an event is so frightening it causes a prolonged alarm reaction, where the body is primed with chemicals like adrenaline and does not calm down for a long time.

Chronic Abuse
Neglect
Attachment Disruption
Loss & Separation
Witnessing Violence
Medical Trauma

⚠️ Critical Difference: Children vs Adults

  • Adults: Trauma alters their mature, organised brain
  • Children: Trauma becomes the primary organising experience, creating the foundation for neurological development
  • Impact: For children, trauma has a detrimental impact on the developing brain itself

🧠 Impact on Brain and Body

⚔️

Fight Response

Aggressive, confrontational

  • Increased adrenaline for strength
  • Ready to defend or attack
  • May appear defiant or hostile
🏃

Flight Response

Avoidant, escaping

  • Body prepared for quick escape
  • May seem restless, fidgety
  • Avoids challenging situations
🧊

Freeze Response

Shutdown, disconnected

  • Endorphins released for numbness
  • Breathing slows down
  • May appear compliant but inattentive

When Trauma Becomes Chronic

  • Chemical reactions stay "switched on" as if never switched off
  • Normal threat responses become permanent states
  • Leads to affect dysregulation - inability to manage emotions
  • Results in problems with anger, impulses, attention, and connection

⚡ Affect Dysregulation

What is Affect Dysregulation?

The inability to regulate emotions and reactions. This capacity is normally built during early years through attuned attachment relationships with well-regulated caregivers.

🔥

Hyperarousal

Reactive, hypervigilant

  • Constantly on high alert
  • Prone to aggression or flight
  • Alarmed by minor triggers
  • Intensely painful and uncomfortable
🌫️

Dissociation

Numb, disconnected

  • Feeling "switched off" or numb
  • Compliant but inattentive
  • Difficulty connecting with others
  • Also intensely painful

The Result: Straight to Reaction

Traumatised children have reduced capacity to regulate strong emotions, leading them straight to reaction with no time to think.

7

🔄 How Trauma Shows Up

Traumatised children tend to re-enact and replicate their trauma through:

  • Fearful reactions to everyday situations
  • Aggressive and sexual acting-out behaviours
  • Avoidance of new experiences or relationships
  • Uncontrolled emotional reactions
  • Viewing adults as potential threats or perpetrators
  • Experiencing rules and protection as punishment

Self-Harm and Addictive Behaviours

Why traumatised children may self-harm:

  • Endorphin seeking: Addicted to stress chemicals
  • Self-hatred: Acting out negative self-image
  • Depression: Wish to end emotional pain
  • Feeling something: Overcoming numbness
  • Self-medication: Attempting to regulate their physiology
  • High-risk behaviours: Seeking stimulation and control

🌱 Intervention and Recovery

1st

Best recovery if intervention in first year of life

Earlier

The earlier intervention, the greater chance of recovery

Hope

Caring adults provide different template of possibilities

Essential Conditions for Recovery

  • Safety First: No recovery unless child feels safe and secure
  • Acknowledged Pain: Adults must acknowledge the hurt they've suffered
  • Appropriate Nurture: Containing difficult behaviours with compassion
  • Held in Mind: Someone who cares, thinks about them, knows how they're feeling
  • Healing Relationships: Positive view from trusted, caring adults
  • Trauma Must Stop: No hope if trauma is still occurring
"To be happy, we all need to know that there is someone who cares about us and thinks about us, thinks about what we are doing, and how we are feeling. This is the basis of security."
